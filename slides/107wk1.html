What is a prototype?

    "Prototypes are provisional models of ideas that help us investigate, communicate, and remember specific aspects of those ideas. A prototype, like a design or a scenario, is a model of something that might exist in the future."(see Ruecker et. al.)

Why prototype in an English class?

  • Building prototypes are initial phases of creating/developing what does not yet exist.
  • "...a new research question is something that does not yet exist. Although some people might argue that research questions are just waiting around to be discovered, in fact they need to be created." (see Ruecker et. al.)
  • Good writing about literature involves creating and exploring questions that do yet exist in response to literary texts. We want to ask new questions. Develop new insights.

An experiment

    Having read Yancey's "Writing in the 21st Century" make a 2-D prototype that can help you and the class think about a key concept or idea in the article and what the implications of this idea or concept might be for writing about literature in the 21st Century.

Process

  • Discuss Yancey's article in small groups. Help each other identify key concepts and ideas in article.
  • Generate initial sketches for 3 different concepts/ideas [individually]
  • Create prototype of single, selected concept/idea [individually]
  • Share prototypes
  • Revise/extend prototype using new materials
